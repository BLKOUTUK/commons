BLKOUT · The Commons · v0.2

Building the
commons.
liberation technology for us, by us.

Who do you work with to reach your audience? How well do you know them? Do you trust them? Should you? When they tell you who they are, believe them.

Corporate platforms extract our data, throttle our reach, and decide quarterly whether our communities are worth keeping. Owning the digital space we organise in is the difference between hosting a conversation and renting permission to have one.

Each of our organisations is small; the tools we need are not. Building them alone burns the time the mission needs. Pooling what we build leaves more capacity for the work — and the things we make together belong to all of us.

The commons is the open question of whether we could be stronger together this time, on purpose.

01 · Heritage

Ivor's Compass

a life's legacy in a different light.

Seven directions drawn from Ivor Cummings's life, built around commissioned essays from four Black queer writers; AI made a one-day workshop feel like six. The Black queer men it reached — most of whom don't come to our events — answered the question of who we weren't reaching.

02 · Frame

Critical Frequency

the data doesn't see us.

The systems that commission services run on data — and the data doesn't see Black queer men. Invisibility in a data-driven system is likely to be fatal. Critical Frequency builds the evidence base that doesn't exist: gathered with consent, in our hands.

// the offer

Dreamcatcher —
and what it actually offers you.

Dreamcatcher is BLKOUT's AI-tool evaluation engine. It works for us because it knows our tech stack and our core values — and that's also why it can't usefully be opened up as software to other organisations. Your stack and your values aren't ours; they shouldn't be.

what travels isn't the tool, it's the pattern.

What we want to share with peer orgs is how an organisation might use AI tools to strengthen its own ethical decision-making — slowly, in its own context, against its own values. Three ways to engage.

// the five questions

Five questions.
Then the ask.

None of this is a bid. It's an honest scan of where peer orgs are starting from. Short answers are perfect. If a question doesn't fit your work, skip it. We'll reply within a week. We won't add you to any list without asking.

1. Who are your people?

2. Who gets missed?

3. What AI tools are you already using?

4. What data invisibility hurts your work most?

5. If we shared one tool across our orgs next quarter, what would be most useful?

6. A specific question on ticketing.
